World Population Maps Intercomparison and Validation:
Data Collection, Processing and Visualization

World population maps provide global information, evidence-based analytics, and knowledge on human presence on the planet. The ultimate goal of these maps is to ensure that every person is mapped and counted within critical operations such as policy making and disasters response. Due to the importance associated with world population maps, the assessment of accuracy and reliability of these datasets is key to inform any users on benefit and limitations deriving from their usage.

For this laboratory we used two raster datasets: The Global Human Settlement and the Worldpop ( for more information about the two maps at the end of the page). The purpose of this lab is to perform two parallel assessments (validation and intercomparison) to check how much the two datasets match. We worked on a limited area called "Area of group 10" shown above in the map.

Tools

GeoServer

GeoServer is an open source server for sharing geospatial data. Designed for interoperability, it publishes data from any major spatial data source using open standards.

OpenLayers

OpenLayers makes it easy to put a dynamic map in any web page. It can display map tiles, vector data and markers loaded from any source. OpenLayers has been developed to further the use of geographic information of all kinds. It is completely free, Open Source JavaScript, released under the 2-clause BSD License (also known as the FreeBSD).

Data

Global Human Settlement Population Grid (GHS-POP)

This spatial raster dataset depicts the distribution and density of population, expressed as the number of people per cell. Global coverage datasets are available to download in Geotiff format with different resolutions and CRS for years 1975, 1900, 2000, and 2015.

WorldPop - Population Counts

Spatial raster dataset with the estimated total number of people per grid-cell. The global coverage dataset is available to download in Geotiff format at a resolution of 30 arcsec (CRS: WGS84) from the Year 2000 to 2020.
